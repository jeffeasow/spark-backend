How to write and handle performance reviews in Indian companies

Performance reviews in Indian corporates typically happen twice a year —
mid year in September or October, and year end in March or April.
The review directly impacts your appraisal rating and salary increment.

Writing your own self appraisal:
Most Indian employees undersell themselves in self appraisals. They list
tasks instead of impact. The difference is critical.

Wrong: "Managed the client account and attended weekly calls"
Right: "Managed the Infosys account end to end, growing revenue by 18%
and maintaining a CSAT score of 4.8 out of 5 across 12 months"

Always quantify where possible. Numbers make impact concrete.
If you cannot find a number, describe the scale or significance.

The STAR format works well for Indian review contexts:
Situation — what was the context
Task — what were you responsible for
Action — specifically what YOU did, not what the team did
Result — what changed because of your action

Writing reviews for your team members:
Be specific and honest. Vague positive feedback like "great team player"
helps nobody. Equally, avoid vague criticism like "needs to improve
communication" without examples.

Good review comment: "Riya led the migration to the new CRM system,
coordinating across 4 teams and delivering 2 weeks ahead of schedule.
Her stakeholder communication was clear throughout — she sent weekly
updates proactively and escalated risks early."

Growth feedback example: "Arjun delivers strong technical work but tends
to wait for direction before starting new tasks. Developing comfort with
ambiguity and proactive ownership will be key for his next level."

Handling your own review conversation:
- Come prepared with 3 specific examples of your impact
- Do not wait for your manager to bring up salary — raise it directly
- If you disagree with a rating, ask for specific examples of where
  you fell short before responding emotionally
- Ask what specifically success looks like at the next level

Common mistakes Indian professionals make in reviews:
- Being too modest in self ratings due to cultural discomfort
- Not documenting achievements throughout the year — scrambling at
  review time to remember what they did
- Accepting vague feedback without asking for clarification
- Not asking directly about promotion timelines
